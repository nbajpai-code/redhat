# Community Blogs & Forums (RHCSA & RHCE)

Join the community to get help, share tips, and stay updated on exam changes for both EX200 and EX294.

## Blogs & Websites

### **[Jeff Geerling's Blog](https://www.jeffgeerling.com/)** (Essential for EX294)
*   **Content:** Deep dives into Ansible, Kubernetes, and homelab setups.
*   **Why Visit:** Jeff Geerling is the "Ansible Guy". His explanations are critical for understanding *why* things work, not just *how*.

### **[LinuxCert Guru](https://linuxcert.guru/)**
*   **Content:** Exam-focused articles, cheat sheets, and updates on EX200/EX294 changes.
*   **Why Visit:** Specific guides on new topics like Flatpak and systemd timers.

### **[RootUsers](https://www.rootusers.com/)**
*   **Content:** High-quality tutorials and study guides.
*   **Why Visit:** Clear examples for complex topics like SELinux and storage management.

### **[CertDepot](https://www.certdepot.net/)** (Historical Archive)
*   **Content:** While older, it remains a legendary resource for RHCSA concepts.
*   **Note:** Ensure you verify commands against RHEL 9/10 documentation as some syntax may have changed.

## Forums & Discussion

### **[r/redhat (Reddit)](https://www.reddit.com/r/redhat/)**
*   **Activity:** Very high.
*   **Why Visit:** "I Passed" posts often contain current exam experiences (without breaking NDA) and resource recommendations.

### **[r/ansible (Reddit)](https://www.reddit.com/r/ansible/)** (Essential for EX294)
*   **Activity:** High.
*   **Why Visit:** The go-to place for Ansible questions, playbook debugging, and best practices.

### **[r/linuxadmin (Reddit)](https://www.reddit.com/r/linuxadmin/)**
*   **Activity:** High.
*   **Why Visit:** General system administration questions and career advice.

### **[Red Hat Learning Community](https://learn.redhat.com/)**
*   **Activity:** Moderate to High.
*   **Why Visit:** Official support and discussion with other students and instructors.
